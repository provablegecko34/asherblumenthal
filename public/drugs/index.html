How to (legally) make DrUgZ

Oct, 2024

The goal of total synthesis is to build complex molecules like morphine from scratch—using small, commercially available molecules such as 3,4-dimethoxybenzaldehyde, methyl vinyl ketone, and simple nitrogen-containing species.

The synthesis can be divided into six strategic stages:

Construction of a functionalized linear precursor
Assembly of the tricyclic phenanthrene core
Asymmetric formation of stereocenters
Creation of the morphine ether bridge
Construction of the piperidine (D) ring
Final deprotections and N-methylation

Step 1: Build the Linear Precursor

We begin with a Michael addition between 3,4-dimethoxybenzaldehyde and methyl vinyl ketone, which creates a 1,5-diketone framework. This sets the stage for ring closures that define morphine’s fused ring system.

Reaction:
Reagents: Methyl vinyl ketone, base (NaOMe or LDA)
Solvent: THF or ethanol
Temperature: 0°C → rt
This intermediate is extended via aldol chemistry and further functionalized to prepare for ring closure.

Step 2: Construct the Phenanthrene Core

With the linear chain in place, an intramolecular aldol condensation is used to close the A/B/C rings—mimicking the morphinan backbone.

Reaction:
Conditions: Acidic or basic aqueous conditions (NaOH, heat)
Protecting groups: Methyl ethers at phenolic positions
This tricyclic phenanthrene is a recognizable morphine scaffold and prepares the molecule for stereocenter installation.

Step 3: Install Stereocenters via Asymmetric Diels-Alder

The next milestone is the asymmetric Diels-Alder reaction, where a chiral auxiliary or catalyst controls the stereochemistry at C9 and C13—two critical centers for morphine's bioactivity.

Diene: Modified enone
Dienophile: Chiral auxiliary or Lewis acid catalyst (e.g., TiCl₄ with a chiral ligand)
Solvent: Dichloromethane
Temperature: -78°C → 0°C
This sets the correct 3D configuration for downstream reactivity.

Step 4: Form the Ether Bridge

Now, we focus on the hallmark ether bridge between C4 and C5. This is accomplished via oxidation and intramolecular cyclization—often via an SN2-type reaction.

Step 1: Oxidize benzylic alcohol to aldehyde (e.g., Dess–Martin periodinane)
Step 2: Cyclize using acid catalysis or base (K₂CO₃, DMF)
This step gives morphine its unique tetrahydrofuran-like E-ring.

Step 5: Construct the Piperidine Ring (D Ring)

The piperidine ring is formed through reductive amination of a ketone with a nitrogen nucleophile, followed by cyclization.

Step 1: Reductive amination with NH₃ or primary amine
Step 2: Cyclization with acid (TsOH) or heating
Step 3: N-methylation with MeI + base (e.g., NaH)
Now we have the full morphinan skeleton, including the D-ring and N-methyl group.

Step 6: Demethylate Phenolic Groups

The final transformation is demethylation of the methoxy groups at C3 and C6 to reveal the active phenols of morphine.

Reagents: BBr₃ or TMSI
Solvent: DCM or acetonitrile
Temperature: -78°C → rt
Controlled demethylation restores the natural hydroxyl groups essential for receptor binding.

Final Product: Morphine

After purification and verification (e.g., NMR, MS), the synthetic morphine is chemically identical to its natural counterpart:

Five fused rings (A–E)
Hydroxyl groups at C3 and C6
Tetrahydrofuran ether bridge
N-methyl piperidine
